# White Lion Question Script

This is a reusable Toronto Zoo Report script built from the About page lens and tuned for the Toronto Zoo white lion talk. Use the public keeper section on the floor, then escalate more sensitive status or welfare questions to management or welfare-science contacts.

## Opening Script

Use a short setup that signals you want keeper-talk answers, not special access.

`I'm documenting the white lion talk for Toronto Zoo Report. I'm mostly looking for public, keeper-talk context about welfare, habitat use, enrichment, and conservation framing.`

## Direct Easy Questions For The Keeper

These are the fastest, most natural follow-up questions to ask on the floor.

- Can you help me tell Lemon and Makali apart?
- Where do they usually choose to spend time on a cold day?
- If they are not out, is that mostly weather, routine, or their own choice?
- About how warm is the house kept in winter?
- What enrichment did they get today or this week?
- What parts of the habitat do they use most?
- What behavior tells you they are relaxed here?
- Is there anything visitors often misread as stress that is actually routine?
- Is there a simple public-safe status update on Lemon today?
- Is there a simple public-safe status update on Makali today?
- What is the biggest misconception visitors have about white lions?

## If The Keeper Has Time, Go A Bit Deeper

These are still keeper-safe, but they take longer and work better after the quick questions.

### Welfare Signals

- What does a normal relaxed day look like for these lions?
- What behavior tells you they are comfortable in the space?
- Are there any behaviors visitors misread as stress when they are actually routine?

### Substrates and Setup

- What ground surfaces do they have access to outside and inside?
- Which parts of the habitat do they choose most often in cold weather?
- What features of this enclosure matter most for older lions?

### Enrichment

- What kinds of enrichment do Lemon and Makali get most often?
- How often is enrichment rotated so it stays interesting?
- Are there scent, feeding, or object enrichments that visitors usually miss?

### Social Management

- How do Lemon and Makali usually use the space together?
- Do they tend to rest together, split up, or alternate favorite spots?
- What does a normal social interaction between them look like?

### Conservation

- What is the most important thing visitors should understand about white lions versus albino lions?
- How do you explain their conservation status in a simple, accurate way for families?
- What public misconception about white lions do you most often correct?

## Toronto Zoo White Lion Add-On

- Is indoor access mainly weather-based, choice-based, or both?
- About how warm is the house kept in winter, and why that temperature range?
- What helps the lions transition comfortably between indoor and outdoor space?
- Which lion is easiest for visitors to identify right now, and how?
- Is there a public-safe update on Lemon's current status?
- Is there a public-safe update on Makali's current status?
- What details about Fintan should visitors know when they ask about the recent family history?
- Are there any age-related adaptations in care for these lions now that they are older adults?

## Habitat Evidence Prompts For Yourself

- Ask the short keeper questions first, then get photos that support or challenge the answers.
- After the answer, take a habitat photo that proves or challenges what you just heard.
- Get one frame showing usable space, one showing substrate, and one showing enrichment.
- If a keeper mentions choice, document the actual choice points: doors, caves, shade, rock, privacy, height, and route options.
- If a keeper mentions enrichment, photograph the item and where it sits in the enclosure.
- Note which lion is visible, where she is resting or moving, and whether the same route gets repeated.

## After You Collect More Info, Escalate To Management Or Welfare Staff

Use these after you have photos, notes, and a few concrete observations. These work better when you can point to something you saw instead of asking in the abstract.

### Grant Furniss, Director of Wildlife Care

- After reviewing these habitat photos, how does Toronto Zoo assess whether the visible public habitat is sufficient for two older adult females?
- What enclosure changes, if any, have been made in response to aging, mobility, or seasonal use patterns?
- How should the blog accurately describe the lions' current institutional program status?
- What public language is acceptable around breeding history, retirement, or long-term housing plans?
- If public visitors see limited enrichment on a given day, what off-view or rotating context is important so the report stays fair?

### Molly McGuire, Manager of Welfare Science

- What welfare indicators are most useful when evaluating older lions in this habitat?
- Based on the route use or door-focused behavior I observed, how does the zoo distinguish routine movement from a stereotypy concern?
- What observations would count as meaningful evidence of positive enclosure use here?
- How do you assess whether substrate, enrichment, and climate support are working well for these individuals?
- If visitors notice repeated squinting or another recurring physical trait, what is the safest way to report that without implying an unconfirmed health problem?

## Status And Health Questions That May Be Deflected

Ask these only when you have the right contact and on-record permission.

- Is there any publicly shareable medical update for Lemon?
- Is there any publicly shareable medical update for Makali?
- Are there mobility, dental, eye, or age-related care changes that can be described on the record?
- Have there been any recent welfare reviews or habitat-use studies specific to these lions?
- Is there any publicly shareable change in access, routine, or behind-the-scenes care that visitors should understand?

## Close

`Thanks. I may follow up with management or welfare-science staff for any health or program questions so I don't overstate anything from the public talk.`
